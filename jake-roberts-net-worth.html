Jake Roberts Wiki Biography

Born on the 30th May 1955 in Gainesville, Texas USA, Aurelian Smith Jr. is a semi-retired professional wrestler, widely known as Jake Roberts through his participation in various wrestling promotions including World Wrestling Federation (now WWE), World Championship Wrestling, and Extreme Championship Wrestling.

So, what’s the net worth of Jake Roberts as of early 2017? Sources estimate his net worth to be $500,000, earned from his successful career in the wrestling industry.

Jake Roberts Net Worth $500,000

Jake, the son of the late wrestler Grizzly Smith, has faced challenges since childhood. Despite the troubled relationship with his father, his journey in wrestling began at a young age. He worked his way up in Mid-South Wrestling, Georgia Championship Wrestling, and Mid-Atlantic Championship Wrestling. He also ventured into the Canadian wrestling scene, where he developed a feud with wrestler Junkyard Dog.

In 1983, Jake became a part of the Legion of Doom and started a feud with Ron Garvin for the NWA World Television Championship. The following year, he moved to World Class Championship Wrestling and, together with Gino Hernandez and “Gentleman” Chris Adams, won the WCCW television title and 6-Man Tag Team title.

His next career move was joining the World Wrestling Federation in 1986. During his time there, he made significant strides, including his memorable pay-per-view debut at WrestleMania 2, and hosting the show “The Snake Pit”. His popularity soared, leading to new opportunities, victories, and an increase in his net worth.

After WWF, he joined World Championship Wrestling in 1992, before transitioning to the Independent circuit in 1993. He then fought for Mexican wrestling promotion Asistencia Sesoria y Administacion and showcased his wrestling skills in Smoky Mountain Wrestling. His journey saw him briefly return to WWF in 1996, followed by stints in Extreme Championship Wrestling and independent promotions.

In 2014, he was inducted into the WWE Hall of Fame in recognition of his wrestling achievements. He was known for his distinctive ring persona and for bringing snakes such as Damien and Lucifer into the ring.

Outside his professional endeavors, Jake has had an eventful personal life – three marriages and eight children, overcoming health challenges including drug and alcohol addiction, cancer, and obesity.

Currently, Jake is retired from active wrestling but continues to make sporadic appearances in the industry.
