Who is Jon Johnson?

Jon Johnson is an American sound editor of television and feature film who has been awarded an Academy Award in the category of Best Sound Editing for his work on the World War II submarine epic ‘U-571’(2000).

Jon Johnson: Professional Life and Career

He started his career as a sound effects editor on animated programs like ‘The New Scooby and Scrappy-Doo Show’ and ‘Superfriends: The Legendary Super Power Show’.

His milestone works include his first sound editor role on the ‘Royal to Mayberry’ in the 1986 reunion special. His major works include the popular science fiction films Star Trek Generations in 1994 and ‘Independence Day in 1996.

Jon Johnson also worked as a sound editor for the Hollywood which includes ‘U-571’ in 2000, ‘A Knight’s Tale’ in 2011, ‘Amazing Grace’ in 2006, ‘The Blind Side’ in 2009, ‘Captain America: The First Avenger’ in 2011, and ‘Saving Mr. Banks in 2013.

He also served as supervising sound editor on the miniseries John Adams in 2008 and the television film ‘Grey Gardens in 2009.

Jon Johnson: Awards, Nominations

Jon Johnson won Emmy Award in the category Outstanding Sound Editing for a Miniseries, Movie, or a Special in 1986 and 2008, won Emmy Awards in the category Outstanding Sound Editing for a Series in 1989, and won Academy Awards in the category Best Sound Editing in 2000.
