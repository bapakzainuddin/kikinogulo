Julie Chen Bio: Age, Parents, Education

Born on 6 January 1970 in New York City to Chinese immigrants parents, Julie considers herself as a true Chinese at heart.  However, it was her Chinese roots she had to forego to have a successful career in the showbiz.

She completed her earlier education from a junior high school in Queens and later attended the St. Francis Preparatory School for graduation. She also graduated from the University of Southern California in 1991 with degrees in Broadcast Journalism and English.

Julie Chen's Career Ladder, Net Worth

Julie in her earlier days interned at CBS Morning News alongside Andy Cohen. The following year she worked for ABC NewsOne as a desk assistant. Later she was promoted to be a producer for the next three years.

From the year 1995 to 1997 she worked as an anchor for WDTN- TV. Chen was also the anchor of CBS Morning News and This Morning starting from 1999 till 2002. She then worked as the co-host of The Early Show on CBS from 2002 to 2010.

It was in 2000 Julie landed her major gig, as the host of the Celebrity Big Brother.  In her first season, Chen was widely criticized for heavily scripted vocabulary during the interview as well as with the audience.

Julie Had Plastic Surgery

In an interview with Life and Style magazine in March 2018, Julie revealed that when she was working as a young reporter (age 25) in Ohio and had to undergo plastic surgery of her eye. Her news director once told Julie that she would never be a top news anchor if she did not change her appearance.

Having aspired to front up a camera since her early age, she did change her appearance through plastic surgery, but the mocking did not stop after the surgery as well. She revealed that after the surgery, she received constant comments like, "Way to give in to the Western standards of beauty," and "You're denying your heritage. You're trying to look less Asian."

However, the constant criticism only served up to fuel her to do well and shut all her critics when she landed one of the most coveted roles of anchoring the Big Brother at CBS.

Julie Chen Married CEO of CBS, Children

And it was at CBS she met the love of her life, Les Moonves, the Chairman of the Board, President, and Chief Executive Officer of CBS Network. 

Julie and Les started dating after his divorce with his first wife; Nancy Wiesenfeld Moonves was finalized in early 2004.  After dating for a short period, Julie tied the knots with her boss Les Moonves on 23rd December 2004.

She got married to Les Moonves in an intimate wedding with not more than 30 guests, composed of only close friends and family in their private home in Acapulco, Mexico.
